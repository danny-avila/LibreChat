============================================================
MINDFUL STRETCHING SESSIONS (EDUCATOR-LED)
============================================================

Mindful stretching sessions are educator-led activities that combine physical movement, narrative imagination, and breath work. Unlike other assignments, these are facilitated sessions -- not tasks assigned for independent completion. Physical space for movement is required. All sessions follow a consistent arc: opening and grounding, journey through challenges, arrival at peace, reflection and return.

IMPORTANT: When generating a lesson plan for an [ES] session, the Facilitation Guide in Section 5 must include the full session narrative script adapted for the specific student -- not just a summary.

------------------------------------------------------------
JOURNEY TO CALM MOUNTAIN
------------------------------------------------------------
Category: Mindful Stretching [ES]
Grade Range: Grades K-12
Activity Type: Educator-Led Session [ES]
Physical Space Required: Open floor space for movement and lying down. Desks should be moved to the perimeter. Yoga mats or floor space recommended for the Summit phase.
Physical Limitations: All poses have seated or modified alternatives. No competitive or performance element -- participation at any level is valid.
Therapeutic Goals: Primary: Calming and immediate self-regulation | Secondary: Grounding and mindfulness; Building self-regulation skills; Improving focus and attention; Body awareness and physical safety

Therapeutic Framing:
The mountain journey narrative works because it maps perfectly onto the emotional experience of regulation: the effort of the climb (managing arousal and challenge), the achievement of the summit (reaching a state of calm), and the descent (returning to the world with something gained). For students who have never experienced a sustained state of calm in their bodies, the Summit phase may be genuinely novel and requires sensitive facilitation -- some students will resist or become anxious as tension releases. The four-phase structure (Warm-Up, Ascend, Summit, Descend) provides the predictability and sequencing that traumatized students need. The Descend and reflection phase is therapeutically important and must not be rushed -- this is where students integrate the experience. The key phrases "you can visit Calm Mountain any time you need peace" plant a self-regulation anchor the student can return to independently.

Description:
A guided mindful stretching journey to Calm Mountain -- using breath, movement, and imagination to find a place of peace and stillness within.

Student-Facing Components:
- Warm-Up: Breathing exercises and gentle stretches to prepare mind and body
- Ascend: Focused stretches building balance and flexibility, symbolizing the climb
- Summit: Deep relaxation and mindfulness at the peak -- the achievement of stillness
- Descend: Gentle cooldown and reflection, returning to the world renewed

Student Instructions:
1. Find a comfortable position and follow the educator's guidance
2. Move at your own pace -- there is no right or wrong way to do this
3. Use the breathing exercises whenever you feel uncomfortable or unsure
4. After the session, notice what feels different in your body and mind

DESERT OASIS ADVENTURE
------------------------------------------------------------
Category: Mindful Stretching [ES]
Grade Range: Grades K-12
Activity Type: Educator-Led Session [ES]
Physical Space Required: Open floor space for movement and lying down. The Shaded Rest pose requires space to lie flat.
Physical Limitations: All poses have seated or standing alternatives. The Shaded Rest lying pose can be done seated with eyes closed. No competitive element.
Therapeutic Goals: Primary: Resilience and coping | Secondary: Calming and immediate self-regulation; Grounding and mindfulness; Hope and future orientation; Body awareness and physical safety

Therapeutic Framing:
The desert oasis metaphor is the most explicitly resilience-focused session in the library. The student crosses a vast, difficult landscape (the trauma experience) to discover that peace and strength are already within them (the oasis). This narrative frame is particularly powerful for students who feel their inner resources have been depleted -- the oasis is not found outside, it is discovered within. The Sun Warrior stretch in the Crossing the Dunes phase gives physical form to the concept of inner strength. The Shaded Rest phase must be given full time -- rushing a student out of deep relaxation defeats the therapeutic purpose. The closing reminder that the oasis is always within the student is the most important moment in the session and should be delivered with care and conviction. This session works particularly well for students who are resilient but exhausted -- who have the strength but have forgotten it is there.

Description:
A guided mindful stretching adventure across an imaginary desert to discover a hidden oasis of inner calm -- a journey that teaches resilience, presence, and the discovery of peace within.

Student-Facing Components:
- Setting Out: Gentle warm-up breathing and stretches, preparing for the journey
- Crossing the Dunes: Balance and strength poses, building resilience as you navigate the landscape
- Discovering the Oasis: Flowing, graceful stretches as the destination comes into view
- Resting in the Shade: Deep relaxation, receiving the peace of the oasis
- Journey Homeward: Gentle return, reflection, and carrying the oasis within

Student Instructions:
1. Follow the educator's guidance and move at your own pace
2. When the journey feels challenging, focus on your breath
3. In the resting phase, let yourself fully arrive -- you have earned this moment
4. After the session, notice what you are carrying with you from the oasis

LIGHTHOUSE BEACON
------------------------------------------------------------
Category: Mindful Stretching [ES]
Grade Range: Grades K-12
Activity Type: Educator-Led Session [ES]
Physical Space Required: Open floor space for movement. The Beacon's Glow meditation can be done seated or lying down.
Physical Limitations: All movements have seated alternatives. Balance poses offer wall support option.
Therapeutic Goals: Primary: Grounding and mindfulness | Secondary: Calming and immediate self-regulation; Self-identity and self-worth; Building trust with adults; Resilience and coping

Therapeutic Framing:
The lighthouse is among the most resonant metaphors in trauma-informed work -- a fixed point of light in darkness, a guide that does not move, a signal that says "you are not alone and you are not lost." For students who feel lost, unseen, or without guidance, the lighthouse narrative speaks directly to their experience. The Lighthouse Twists -- turning to look for the beacon -- physicalize the act of seeking orientation and perspective. The Beacon's Glow meditation is the emotional climax of the session: students experience the light enveloping them, which is a direct somatic experience of safety and being held. The closing affirmation that the beacon is always within reach plants a self-regulation anchor. This session is particularly appropriate for students who are in the midst of difficult transitions or who feel they have lost direction.

Description:
A guided mindful stretching voyage to a lighthouse -- using movement, breath, and imagination to find guidance, safety, and inner light.

Student-Facing Components:
- Setting Sail: Seated breathing and opening stretches, beginning the journey
- Navigating the Waves: Flowing arm sweeps synchronized with breath, moving through challenges
- Guiding Light: Lighthouse Twists seeking perspective and orientation
- Reaching the Shore: Grounded walking steps arriving at stability
- Ascending the Lighthouse: Mindful climbing movement toward clarity
- Beacon's Glow: Restorative meditation in the lighthouse light, safety and accomplishment

Student Instructions:
1. Follow the educator's guidance and let the story carry you
2. When the ocean feels rough, breathe slowly and find the lighthouse
3. In the Beacon's Glow, let yourself rest fully in that light
4. Remember that the lighthouse -- your inner guide -- is always there when you need it
