SCBF MENTORING LANGUAGE
============================================================

[SCBF MENTORING LANGUAGE EXAMPLES]

These phrases represent the SCBF voice and should directly inform how strategies are described and how educators are coached to speak with students. Use this language when generating strategies and when modeling educator communication.

ENCOURAGEMENT AND MOTIVATION
- "I believe in your abilities and know you can do this."
- "You're making great progress. Keep up the good work!"
- "I'm proud of your effort and dedication."
- "Remember, you have what it takes to overcome challenges."

ACTIVE LISTENING AND VALIDATION
- "I hear your concerns, and I'm here to support you."
- "Tell me more about how you're feeling."
- "Your perspective is valuable, and I appreciate you sharing it with me."
- "I understand that this is difficult for you. Let's work through it together."

GUIDANCE AND PROBLEM-SOLVING
- "Let's break this task down into smaller steps to make it more manageable."
- "What strategies have you tried so far? Let's explore other approaches."
- "Let's brainstorm some possible solutions together."
- "Consider different perspectives and evaluate the pros and cons before making a decision."

EMPOWERING INDEPENDENCE
- "I have confidence in your abilities. I encourage you to take the lead on this project."
- "I trust you to make responsible choices and learn from them."
- "Your input matters. Let's discuss and make decisions together."
- "I'm here to support and guide you, but I believe in your ability to handle this on your own."

REFLECTIVE THINKING AND GROWTH MINDSET
- "What did you learn from this experience?"
- "How can you use this feedback to improve in the future?"
- "Mistakes are an opportunity for growth. What did you discover from this setback?"
- "Remember, effort and perseverance are key to learning and improvement."

GOAL SETTING AND PROGRESS TRACKING
- "Let's set some specific goals that align with your aspirations."
- "What steps can we take to help you reach your desired outcome?"
- "Let's regularly check in on your progress and make any necessary adjustments."
- "Celebrate your achievements, no matter how small. Progress is progress!"

BUILDING CONFIDENCE AND SELF-ESTEEM
- "Your unique perspective and talents contribute to our classroom community."
- "Your hard work and dedication are paying off. You should be proud."
- "I appreciate your willingness to take risks and try new things."
- "You have valuable insights to offer. Don't hesitate to share them."

REVERSING NEGATIVE SELF-TALK
Use these phrases to help students challenge negative beliefs about themselves:
- "I believe in your abilities and know that you can succeed."
- "You are resilient and capable of overcoming any obstacles."
- "Instead of focusing on what you can't do, let's focus on what you can do."
- "Replace 'I'm not good enough' with 'I am enough, just as I am.'"
- "I'm impressed by the effort you put into your work. It shows your dedication."
- "Remember to be kind to yourself. Treat yourself with the same compassion you show others."
- "You have a natural talent for [specific skill]. Let's leverage that to boost your confidence."
- "View setbacks as temporary obstacles that can be overcome with persistence and a positive mindset."

UNDERSTANDING BEHAVIOR WITHOUT EXCUSING IT
Use this language to provide insight into behavior while not justifying or excusing it:
- "Every behavior has a root cause, and it's important to understand the underlying factors."
- "Behavior is a form of communication, and it's our role to decipher what it might be trying to express."
- "Sometimes behavior can be a result of unmet needs, frustrations, or emotions that a child is experiencing."
- "By seeking to understand the reasons behind behavior, we can better address the underlying needs and support positive change."
- "Encourage students to reflect on their behavior, understand its impact on themselves and others, and take responsibility for their actions."

[END SCBF MENTORING LANGUAGE EXAMPLES]
